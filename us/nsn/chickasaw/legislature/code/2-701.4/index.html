Chickasaw Nation Code

Section 2-701.4 GENERAL RULES OF PROCEDURE.

A. Unless otherwise provided by Chickasaw law, no action shall be brought more than two (2) years after such cause of action has occurred or been discovered, whichever occurs later.

B. All Commission matters are confidential unless otherwise required or allowed by law or deemed by the Commission to be in the best interest of the Chickasaw Nation.

C. Complaints must be submitted to the Commission in writing and include the following: the Complainant's name and contact information; a statement of the facts; the date(s) of the alleged violation(s); a reference to the ethics provision(s) believed to have been violated; the name of the Respondent(s); the name and contact information of any potential witnesses and a brief statement of their knowledge; and certification from the Complainant that they have personal knowledge of the facts alleged and the information provided in the complaint is true and accurate to the best of their ability.

D. The Commission shall provide notice of an administrative hearing to the parties to a hearing via certified mail, return receipt requested and posted no later than ten (10) business days in advance of the hearing. The notice shall include the hearing date, time and location, as well as the reason for the hearing.

E. Failure to appear at a scheduled hearing without good cause may result in a dismissal of the complaint or the entering of a decision without further proceedings.

F. A Complainant or their legal representative shall present the complaint. Complainant(s) and Respondent(s) who are the subject of ethics complaint(s) have the right to present evidence, to testify on their own behalf, question witnesses against them and call witnesses to testify in support of their respective causes.

G. The burden of proof shall be on the Complainant and decisions will be made according to preponderance of the evidence.

H. Two (2) Commissioners shall constitute a quorum; decisions shall be made by majority vote. In the event only two (2) Commissioners are present, decisions shall be made by unanimous vote. When a required Recusal of one or more Commissioners inhibits the Commission's ability to function, the Governor may invoke a necessity exception, either allowing the participation of any recused Commissioner in the matter or temporarily appointing one or more alternate Commissioners to address the specific matter.

(PR43-001, 02/20/2026.)
