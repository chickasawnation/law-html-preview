Chickasaw Nation Code

Section 5-501.4 INITIAL APPEARANCE.

A. Initial Appearance. Initial Appearance is the bringing of an accused person before the Court, informing them of the charge(s) against them and of their rights, receiving their plea and setting bail. Initial Appearances shall be held in open court wherein the accused person may appear in-person or by video or audio conference technology, as approved by the District Court. If the accused was arrested in response to a warrant and confined, the Initial Appearance shall occur within seventy-two (72) hours of the arrest, Saturdays, Sundays and legal holidays excepted. If the accused is arrested without a warrant and confined, the arresting law enforcement officer must complete an Affidavit of Probable Cause to Make Warrantless Custodial Arrest and present it to a judicial officer within forty-eight (48) hours for determination of probable cause to detain the accused until arraignment is held. The Court shall maintain a record of all Initial Appearances in conformity with 5 § 5-202.13.

B. Procedure at Initial Appearance. Unless waived by the Defendant, the Initial Appearance shall be conducted in the following order:

1. The Court shall ascertain whether the identifying information of the accused person, as indicated in the caption of the complaint, is accurate and correct. The Court or prosecutor should read all charge(s) set forth in the complaint alleged against the accused, inclusive of the minimum and maximum punishment allowed by law. The Court shall ensure that the Office of the Prosecutor has provided a true and accurate copy of the Complaint to the Defendant.

2. The Court should determine that the accused understands the nature of the charge(s) alleged against him and shall explain to the Defendant that he or she has the following rights:

a. The Defendant is presumed to be innocent of all charges.

b. That the Chickasaw Nation bears the burden to establish the Defendant's guilt of the alleged offenses beyond a reasonable doubt.

c. The Defendant has the right to remain silent and the right against self-incrimination.

d. The Defendant is entitled to a speedy and public trial. The Defendant has the right to request a trial before a jury comprised of six (6) persons for all offenses in which incarceration is a possible punishment.

e. The right to hear, see, and confront all witnesses called to testify against him.

f. The right to present evidence on his own behalf and the right to compel witnesses to appear before the Court to testify on his own behalf.

g. The right to consult with an attorney prior to the disposition of the case. The right to have an attorney represent the Defendant at all stages of the case; and, for all offenses in which incarceration is a possible punishment, the right to request a court-appointed attorney if the defendant is determined to be indigent by the Court.

h. The right to file a Writ of Habeas Corpus with the Federal District Court to determine the legality of the Chickasaw Nation's detention under 25 U.S.C. §1303.

3. The Defendant should be advised that requests for court-appointed counsel shall be made on a form approved by the District Court for that purpose. If the Court determines that the Defendant is indigent, a licensed attorney will be appointed to represent the Defendant.

C. Receipt of Plea at Initial Appearance. Once the Defendant has been properly advised of all rights afforded to him under the law, as set forth above, the Court should inquire whether the Defendant desires to enter a plea of not guilty, nolo contendere, or guilty.

1. If the Defendant refuses to plead, the Court shall enter a plea of not guilty on his behalf.

2. If the Defendant is uncertain how to plead, the Court shall enter a plea of not guilty on his behalf.

3. If the Defendant pleads not guilty, the Court shall set a future hearing date, affix bail for bailable offenses, and set any special conditions for bail prior to trial.

4. If the Defendant pleads nolo contendere or guilty, the Court shall conduct an inquiry to determine the following:

a. The Defendant understands the nature of the charges and the applicable range of punishment for each offense and that the Court may impose sentencing within the range of punishment for each offense, as set forth by law;

b. That by entering a plea of nolo contendere or guilty, the Defendant is waiving all rights, as set forth above;

c. That the Defendant desires to enter such plea voluntarily without coercion.

5. If the Court accepts the plea of nolo contendere or guilty, the Court may impose sentencing in conformity with 5 § 5-701.2 et seq.

6. If the Defendant desires to enter a plea of nolo contendere or guilty to a felony offense or to an enhanceable misdemeanor offense, the Court shall cause a verbatim record to be made of the plea by digital, electronic, or stenographic means, as approved by the District Court in conformity with 5 § 5-202.13 et seq., unless expressly waived by the Defendant and the Chickasaw Nation.
