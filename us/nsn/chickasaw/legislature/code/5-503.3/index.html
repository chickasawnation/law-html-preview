Chickasaw Nation Code

Section 5-503.3 BURDEN OF PROOF AT PRELIMINARY HEARING.

A. The purpose of a Preliminary Hearing is to determine whether probable cause exists to believe an alleged crime has occurred, and whether probable cause exists to believe the defendant committed that crime. The Chickasaw Nation bears the burden of proof at a Preliminary Hearing, and is entitled to the presumption that it will strengthen its evidence at trial.

B. The Special Judge may find probable cause to bind the defendant over on the charges as filed by the Chickasaw Nation, to amend the charges based on the evidence presented, or to find the defendant committed different or additional felony charges, based on the evidence presented at the Preliminary Hearing. If the Special Judge makes such a determination, the Special Judge must include the new or amended charges in the Order and advise the defendant of the new charges and the maximum range of punishment for each charge. The Defendant shall not be entitled to a new Preliminary Hearing or Initial Appearance for any amended or additional charge added by the Special Judge after a Preliminary Hearing. The Chickasaw Nation shall submit an amended complaint in conformance with the Special Judge's findings within five (5) working days of the Preliminary Hearing.

(PR41-016, 9/20/2024.)
